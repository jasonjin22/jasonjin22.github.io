This is a review of chapter 4 of Introduciton to Probability models(\(11^{th}\) edition), here I will focus on some important examples and concepts.

Chapman-Kolmogorov Equations

  • \(P_{i,j}^{n+m} = \sum\limits _{k=0}^{\infty}P_{ik}^{n}P_{kj}^{m}, n \ge 0, i, j, \ge 0\)

  • Example 4.11 Suppose that balls are successively distributed among 8 urns, with each ball being equally likely to be put in any of these urns. What is the probability that there will be exactly 3 nonempty urns after 9 balls have been distributed?
    • Let \(X_n\) to denote # of filled urns after n balls are dropped
    • \(X_n = \{0, 1, 2, ..., 8\}\)
    • we want \(P_{0,3}^9 = P_{1,3}^8\), where \(X_n = \{1, 2, 3, 4\}\)
